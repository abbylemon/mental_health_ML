Mental Health in Tech

Using data to improve mental health conversations in the workplace.

Overview Top

The Open Sourcing Mental Illness (OSMI) Mental Health in Tech survey includes categorical data about employees’ attitudes toward mental health in the workplace in the tech industry. For this project, data was used to compare and contrast different machine learning classification models. The goal of this project was to see if there is a model that can classify whether an employee would seek treatment for mental health based on different workplace features/factors.

Background Top

OSMI was founded in 2013 as a response to an observed lack of mental health resources for those working within the tech community. Since 2014, annual surveys have been conducted to capture the attitudes, perceptions, and frequency of mental health disorders within this group. This research has led to documentation meant to assist employers in developing supporting environments for those with mental health disorders, as well as general awareness through presenting at conferences and company events.

If you are part of the tech and open source community and would like to contribute to OSMI’s research, the 2020 Mental Health in Tech survey is now available.
